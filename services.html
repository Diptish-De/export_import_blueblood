Why Source Traditional Indian Artefacts from Us?

India holds a wealth of cultural heritage, beautifully expressed through its traditional handcrafted materials. As a government-recognized exporter (OPC Private Limited), Blueblood Exports connects global buyers with incredible artisan communities across 15 states in India.

Whether you are an interior designer seeking bespoke décor, a retail gallery owner buying wholesale Indian handicrafts, or a collector of rare brass and bronze sculptures, we provide end-to-end export services.

  • Authentic Craftsmanship: Handcrafted using techniques passed down for generations.
  • Premium Export Quality: Rigorous multi-stage QC ensures flawless, export-ready pieces.
  • Ethical B2B Sourcing: Direct collaboration with over 200 master artisan families.

Frequently Asked Questions About Importing Artefacts

What traditional materials do you export from India?
We export a wide range of premium handcrafted artefacts made from traditional Indian materials, including lost-wax brass and bronze castings, intricately carved teak and sandalwood, authentic handloomed silk and cotton textiles, and expertly sculpted Makrana marble and stone crafts.
Do you accept wholesale or bulk export orders for handicrafts?
Yes, Blueblood Exports specializes in B2B wholesale and bulk export shipments for retailers, interior designers, gallery owners, and large collectors globally. We provide scalable production while maintaining strict export-quality standards.
How do you ensure the quality of export artefacts?
Every artefact goes through a multi-stage quality control process. We inspect for material authenticity, craftsmanship details, structural integrity, and exquisite finishing to ensure all goods meet premium international export standards before secure packaging.
To which countries do you export Indian handicrafts?
We have a robust global logistics network and export to numerous countries across the Americas, Europe, the Middle East, and Asia-Pacific. Major markets include the USA, UK, Germany, UAE, Canada, France, Japan, and Australia.
